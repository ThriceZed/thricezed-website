360° HDRI Capture

Shoot a full 360° high-dynamic-range environment map with just your phone — the kind of HDRI you drop into Blender, Maya, or Unreal to light a CGI scene with real-world lighting. The tool guides you like Google Street View: hold up your phone, follow the dots around you, and it captures and stitches the sphere automatically.

Launch the tool Back to Software
01

Aim & follow

Point your phone's rear camera at the world. Floating target dots appear around you — line the reticle up with each one.

02

Auto-capture

When you're on target, the tool snaps the tile automatically — bracketing exposures where your device allows it, so highlights and shadows both survive.

03

Stitch & export

It reprojects every tile into a seamless equirectangular sphere and hands you a true .hdr file, ready for your 3D software.

What you'll need. A phone with a rear camera and motion sensors, opened over HTTPS (this page qualifies). Hold the phone in portrait and turn your whole body to sweep the scene.

About true HDR. Genuine high-dynamic-range needs multiple exposures per angle. Android Chrome generally allows the tool to bracket exposures for real HDR. On iPhone, Safari does not expose camera-exposure control to web apps, so iOS captures a single exposure per tile — still a valid .hdr, but with a narrower dynamic range. For hero shots, a dedicated bracketing camera app will always win; this is built for speed and convenience on location.

Tip. Keep the camera roughly over the same spot as you turn, avoid moving people or cars, and lock focus by staying still for a beat on each dot.
